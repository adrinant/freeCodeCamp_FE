Ir. Soekarno

Here's the timeline of Ir. Soekarno's life:

  • 1901 - Soekarno was born on June 6 in Surabaya, Dutch East Indies (now Indonesia).
  • 1920 - Soekarno moved to Bandung to study at the Technische Hoogeschool (Technical University).
  • 1927 - Soekarno became involved in nationalist activities and joined the Indonesian National Party (PNI).
  • 1928 - Soekarno and other nationalists issued the Youth Pledge, expressing the desire for one independent nation, one people, and one language.
  • 1930 - Soekarno was arrested and imprisoned by the Dutch colonial authorities for his nationalist activities.
  • 1942 - During World War II, Japan occupied the Dutch East Indies. Soekarno cooperated with the Japanese authorities in the hope of gaining Indonesian independence.
  • 1945 - On August 17, Soekarno and Mohammad Hatta declared Indonesia's independence, just days after Japan's surrender.
  • 1949 - The Dutch attempted to re-establish control over Indonesia, leading to the Indonesian National Revolution. The Dutch-Indonesian Round Table Conference resulted in the recognition of Indonesian independence in December.
  • 1950-1957 - Soekarno served as the first President of Indonesia.
  • 1957 - Soekarno implemented "Guided Democracy," consolidating his power.
  • 1965 - An attempted coup led to political turmoil. Soekarno was eventually stripped of power, and General Suharto emerged as the de facto leader.
  • 1967 - Soekarno was officially replaced by Suharto as president.
  • 1970 - Soekarno passed away on June 21 in Jakarta, Indonesia.
